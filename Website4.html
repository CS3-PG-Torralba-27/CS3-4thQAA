What are Cybersecurity threats and their types?

Cybersecurity threats encompass a wide range of malicious activities that aim to compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of computer systems, networks, and data.

Here are some essential cybersecurity measures:

Malware:

Malicious software designed to infiltrate or damage a computer system. This includes viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware, spyware, and adware.

Phishing:

Phishing attacks involve tricking users into providing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data by posing as a trustworthy entity in an email or other communication.

Denial-of-Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS):

DoS attacks overwhelm a system with traffic or requests, causing it to become slow or unavailable. DDoS attacks involve multiple sources attacking a single target simultaneously, making it even harder to mitigate.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM):

In MitM attacks, an attacker intercepts communication between two parties, potentially eavesdropping on or altering the data transmitted.

SQL Injection:

SQL Injection attacks exploit vulnerabilities in web applications to inject malicious SQL code into a query, enabling attackers to access, modify, or delete data from databases.

Zero-Day Exploits:

Zero-Day Exploits target vulnerabilities in software or hardware that are unknown to the vendor or developers. Attackers exploit these vulnerabilities before a fix or patch is available.

Insider Threats:

Insider Threats involve malicious actions or data breaches perpetrated by individuals within an organization, such as employees, contractors, or business partners.

Social Engineering:

Social Engineering attacks manipulate individuals into divulging confidential information or performing actions that compromise security, often through psychological manipulation or deception.

Cryptojacking:

Cryptojacking involves hijacking a victim's computing resources to mine cryptocurrencies without their consent or knowledge.

IoT Vulnerabilities:

Internet of Things (IoT) devices often lack robust security measures, making them vulnerable to attacks that exploit weaknesses to gain unauthorized access or control.

Supply Chain Attacks:

Supply Chain Attacks target the software supply chain, aiming to compromise software updates or dependencies to distribute malware or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Password Attacks

Password attacks involve various methods, such as brute force or dictionary attacks, to guess or steal passwords and gain unauthorized access to accounts or systems.
